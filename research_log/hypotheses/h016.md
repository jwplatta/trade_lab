## Raw Question

Can a partial repair that removes only the tested spread keep enough residual edge
to outperform a hard exit while still reducing the worst tail behavior?

## Notes

- Actor: The shared 7DTE SPXW iron condor engine with a tested-side-only repair.
- Constraint: The strategy must use the same trigger definition and initial entry logic as the other variants.
- Mechanism: On trigger, buy back the tested vertical and leave the untested side open through expiry.
- Persistence: The approach should help in repeated one-sided moves where the untested side still has decay value after the stressed side is removed.
- Kill condition: The remaining naked-directional vertical contributes too little premium or introduces new path risk that offsets any benefit from reducing convexity.

## Primary Prediction

Partial close should land between hard exit and baseline on tail metrics, while
retaining more post-trigger expectancy than a full-position close.

## Secondary Predictions

- The strategy may do best on triggers that occur relatively early, leaving enough time for the untested spread to decay.
- Partial close may underperform on sharp reversals if the remaining spread becomes the newly tested side after adjustment.
