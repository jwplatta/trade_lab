## Raw Question

Does immediate risk elimination improve portfolio-level drawdown enough to justify
giving up any chance of post-trigger recovery?

## Notes

- Actor: The shared 7DTE SPXW iron condor entry model with a full-position exit as the only repair action.
- Constraint: The same trigger definition, entry selection, and expiry logic as the control must remain unchanged.
- Mechanism: When the tested side is stressed, close the entire condor and stop participating in any further path on that trade.
- Persistence: Tail-risk reduction should appear across multiple adverse episodes, not just one isolated volatility shock.
- Kill condition: Hard exit reduces expectancy and trade recovery too much without creating a meaningful improvement in worst-loss concentration or drawdown depth.

## Primary Prediction

Hard exit should produce the shallowest post-trigger loss distribution and the
smallest maximum additional drawdown after trigger among the repair variants.

## Secondary Predictions

- Hard exit will likely show more frequent realized medium losses than baseline.
- Time-to-recovery at the portfolio level may improve if large tail losses are prevented early enough.
