## Raw Question

Is a high-DTE hard close rule a cleaner way to cut left-tail risk than trying to
repair the position with a same-expiry recenter?

## Notes

- Actor: A single-position 7DTE SPXW iron condor strategy using the baseline `IronCondorFinder`.
- Constraint: Keep the baseline entry timing, pricing, and expiry-selection rules unchanged.
- Mechanism: If short-leg delta reaches `0.20` while the trade is in the `6-7` DTE bucket, close the condor outright instead of holding or repairing.
- Persistence: The rule should improve downside behavior across different volatility regimes, not only isolated stress periods.
- Kill condition: The early close reduces return and trade count without materially improving drawdown or loss concentration versus baseline and exp01.

## Primary Prediction

The high-DTE close rule should improve drawdown and worst-loss severity relative
to the baseline because it truncates adverse excursions earlier in the trade lifecycle.

## Secondary Predictions

- The close rule will likely have lower net profit than the recenter rule because it gives up time value.
- If this rule helps, the benefit should show up more clearly in tail metrics than in Sharpe or win rate.
