## Raw Question

Is adding time after a trigger a better way to repair a 7DTE condor than either
closing it outright or recentering on the same expiry?

## Notes

- Actor: The shared 7DTE SPXW iron condor engine with a roll-forward repair to a fresh valid ~7DTE expiry from the trigger date.
- Constraint: The trigger definition and baseline entry rules must remain identical to the other variants.
- Mechanism: On trigger, close the existing condor and reopen a replacement condor with similar structure on the next valid baseline-style expiry.
- Persistence: Improvement should show up in multiple selloffs and trend moves where extra time reduces late-cycle convexity.
- Kill condition: Roll-forward extends exposure and order count without a corresponding improvement in tail severity, drawdown profile, or recovery odds.

## Primary Prediction

Roll-forward should reduce post-trigger tail damage more than baseline and same-expiry
recenter by moving the trade away from the most fragile part of the expiry curve.

## Secondary Predictions

- Roll-forward may retain more expectancy than hard exit by preserving a path to renewed credit collection.
- The rule will likely lengthen effective holding time and increase turnover relative to the control.
