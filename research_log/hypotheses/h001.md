## Raw Question

Does a same-expiry recenter improve drawdown and worst-loss behavior when a
7DTE SPXW iron condor reaches short-leg delta 0.20 while still at 6 to 7 DTE?

## Notes

- Actor: A single-position 7DTE SPXW iron condor strategy using the baseline `IronCondorFinder`.
- Constraint: Hold the baseline entry cash, DTE, pricing, and order-refresh constants fixed.
- Mechanism: Close the live condor and re-enter a fresh condor on the same expiry when short delta reaches `0.20` in the `6-7` DTE bucket.
- Persistence: The rule should help across different volatility regimes, not only in isolated stress periods.
- Kill condition: Recentering adds turnover without materially improving drawdown or loss concentration versus baseline.

## Primary Prediction

The high-DTE same-expiry recenter should reduce left-tail severity more than the
baseline hold behavior because it resets the condor farther from spot before the
trade reaches the more fragile middle and late DTE buckets.

## Secondary Predictions

- The recenter rule will increase turnover and fees relative to baseline.
- If the rule helps, the benefit should appear more clearly in drawdown and worst-loss concentration than in win rate.
