## Change Tested

Tested experiment 3 from the repair matrix: when a live 7DTE SPXW iron condor
reaches short-leg delta `>= 0.20` while in the `3-5` DTE bucket, the strategy
closes the position and re-enters a fresh iron condor on the same expiry.

## Method

Ran the standalone project `qc/spxw_7dte_exp03_recenter_mid_dte` in
QuantConnect cloud using minute SPX and SPXW data, the baseline entry
constants, and the same `IronCondorFinder` as the no-constraints baseline.
Because the backtest terminated with a runtime error, no downloaded results
JSON was generated. The metrics in this entry were transcribed from the
QuantConnect results screenshot instead of parsed from a saved payload.

## Interpretation

This result is strong enough not to need a rerun. Mid-DTE same-expiry recenter
did not merely underperform; it effectively failed under stress. The April 2025
tariff regime appears to have produced a steady stream of losses until the
strategy nearly exhausted buying power, after which the backtest stopped. That
is direct evidence against the hypothesis that this repair improves the left
tail.

## Artifacts And Caveats

- Suspicious artifacts: none beyond the fact that the run terminated early rather than completing the full date range.
- Data issues: no saved QuantConnect results JSON exists because the backtest errored out before a normal exportable result payload was produced.
- Execution anomalies: QuantConnect reported `Runtime Error: Sequence contains no matching element in OptionStrategyPositionGroupBuyingPowerModel.cs:line 498`.
- Market regime note: the largest damage appears to have occurred during the April 2025 tariff shock described in the run notes.

## Next Step

Treat experiment 3 as rejected and move to `exp04` to test whether a mid-DTE
hard close behaves better than mid-DTE same-expiry recentering.
